So, you want to join the WIRED committee for 2015-16...

Getting involved with WIRED Monash will help you impress your future employers and boost your chances in getting a job. We will help you develop your leadership, communication, and event organising skills. In addition, if you are a creative person (like a photographer or graphic designer), we will give you the opportunity to help build up your portfolio and showcase your skills.

In order to contest one of the office bearer positions, you must attend the AGM in order to be nominated. However, you can express your interest in nominating by filling in the form below.

Expression of Interest Form

The Returning Officer for this election is Terence Huynh.

Positions Available

President

The duties of the President shall be to

  1. preside as chair over committee meetings and General Meetings;
  2. attend meetings of the Clubs & Societies Council, vote in the interests of the club, and report outcomes to the committee;
  3. co-ordinate the activities of the committee;
  4. represent the club, and act as its spokesperson, to the Clubs & Societies Council, MSA, the University and other bodies, as appropriate;
  5. submit reports to committee meetings, as appropriate; and
  6. submit an annual report to the AGM.

If the constitutional amendment passes, then the President will also

  1. provide a comprehensive handover, including a detailed operational report, at the end of term for the next position holder.

Vice President

The duties of the President shall be to

  1. attend committee meetings and General Meetings;
  2. preside as chair over committee meetings and General Meetings, in the absence of the President;
  3. assist the President in her/his duties;
  4. perform the duties of the President if that office is vacant; and
  5. perform other duties, as the committee may resolve.

If the constitutional amendment passes, then the Vice President will also

  1. hold the diversity portfolio, that is to act as the voice of equal opportunity and diversity on all decisions and at all club events;
  2. liaise with the Engineering and IT faculties, similar student groups, and the greater IT industry to promote diversity to members and the wider student body; and
  3. provide a comprehensive handover, including a detailed operational report, at the end of term for the next position holder.

Treasurer

The duties of the President shall be to

  1. attend committee meetings and General Meetings;
  2. keep the club’s financial books in order, in accordance with the requirements of the Clubs & Societies Council;
  3. prepare a club budget, at least annually;
  4. collect and bank all income in the club’s bank account;
  5. prepare the club’s financial books for audit, semi-annually;
  6. submit a financial report to the AGM; and
  7. liaise with the Clubs & Societies Council on any financial requirements.

If the constitutional amendment passes, then the Treasurer will also

  1. provide a comprehensive handover, including a detailed operational report, at the end of term for the next position holder.

Secretary

The duties of the President shall be to

  1. attend committee meetings and General Meetings;
  2. prepare agendas and minutes, and serve notice as prescribed, for General Meetings and committee meetings;
  3. act as Returning Officer for club elections, as appropriate;
  4. ensure that an up-to-date membership register is maintained;
  5. regularly check the club's mail box and electronic mail account, supplied by the Clubs & Societies Council, for incoming correspondence;
  6. ensure that inwards and outwards correspondence for the club is handled correctly and in a timely manner; and
  7. submit an annual report to the AGM.

If the constitutional amendment passes, then the Secretary will also

  1. maintain the club's social media accounts and website; and
  2. provide a comprehensive handover, including a detailed operational report, at the end of term for the next position holder.

Industry Officer

Note: This position will be open only if the constitutional amendment pass.

The duties of the Industry Officer shall be to

  1. attend committee meetings and General Meetings;
  2. organise sponsorship for the club and events, and ensure all sponsorship activities are met;
  3. connect the club and students with the IT industry and professional bodies;
  4. liaise with the Activities Officer with the organisation of industry events;
  5. provide a comprehensive handover, including a detailed operational report, at the end of term for the next position holder; and
  6. perform other duties, as the committee may resolve.

Education Officer

Note: This position will be open only if the constitutional amendment pass.

The duties of the Education Officer shall be to

  1. attend committee meetings and General Meetings;
  2. liaise, collaborate and maintain contact with staff of the Engineering and IT faculties;
  3. create, organise and promote opportunities for members across all faculties to learn or gain knowledge;
  4. provide a comprehensive handover, including a detailed operational report, at the end of term for the next position holder; and
  5. perform other duties, as the committee may resolve.

Activities Officer

Note: This position will be open only if the constitutional amendment pass.

The duties of the Activities Officer shall be to

  1. attend committee meetings and General Meetings;
  2. organise the club’s BBQs;
  3. develop and organise club events;
  4. liaise with the Industry Officer with the organisation of industry events;
  5. provide a comprehensive handover, including a detailed operational report, at the end of term for the next position holder; and
  6. perform other duties, as the committee may resolve.

General Representative (x 3)

The duties of the General Representative shall be to perform such duties as the committee may resolve.

This role is currently titled "Member without Portfolio". This will be renamed as General Representative only if the constitutional amendments pass.
